## Why Meta Ads?

With over 3 billion monthly users across Facebook and Instagram, **Meta Ads** give small businesses access to one of the most powerful advertising platforms in the world — at a fraction of what traditional advertising costs.

## How Meta Ads Work

Unlike search ads where people come to you, Meta Ads put your brand in front of people who **didn't know they needed you yet**. It's interruption-based, but done right, it's incredibly powerful.

**Meta's targeting lets you reach people by:**
- Location, age, and demographics
- Interests and behaviours
- Lookalike audiences (people similar to your best customers)
- Website visitors (retargeting)

## Setting Up Your First Campaign

### 1. Choose the Right Objective

Meta offers several campaign objectives. For small businesses, start with:
- **Traffic** — drive people to your website
- **Leads** — collect contact information directly
- **Sales** — for e-commerce conversions

### 2. Define Your Audience

Don't target everyone. Start narrow with a well-defined audience of 500K–2M people. You can always broaden later once you find what works.

### 3. Create Scroll-Stopping Creatives

Your ad creative is everything. The best targeting in the world won't save a boring ad.

**What works in 2026:**
- Short-form video (Reels-style, 15–30 seconds)
- Before/after transformations
- Customer testimonials
- Bold text overlays with clear value propositions

### 4. Set a Realistic Budget

Start with ₹500–1000 per day. This gives Meta's algorithm enough data to optimise. Running ₹100/day won't generate meaningful results.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Boosting posts instead of running proper campaigns** — Boosting is Meta's simplified tool with limited targeting. Always use Ads Manager.
- **Changing ads too frequently** — Give each ad set at least 3-5 days before making changes. The algorithm needs time to learn.
- **Ignoring the pixel** — Install the Meta Pixel on your website from day one. It tracks conversions and builds retargeting audiences.
- **No clear call to action** — Every ad should tell the viewer exactly what to do next.

## Measuring Success

Track these metrics weekly:
- **CTR (Click-Through Rate)** — aim for 1%+
- **CPC (Cost Per Click)** — varies by industry, but lower is better
- **ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)** — your revenue divided by ad spend
- **Frequency** — if above 3, your audience is seeing the ad too often

## The Bottom Line

Meta Ads are one of the most cost-effective ways for small businesses to grow online. Start small, test different creatives, track your results, and scale what works.
