## The Eternal Debate

"Should I run Google Ads or Facebook/Instagram Ads?" — it's the question we get asked most. The answer? It depends on your business, audience, and goals.

## Google Ads: Capture Existing Demand

Google Ads works best when people are **actively searching** for what you offer. Someone Googles "best digital marketing agency in Mumbai" — your ad appears at the top.

**Best for:**
- Service businesses (lawyers, doctors, agencies)
- E-commerce with specific product searches
- Local businesses
- High-intent keywords

**Pros:** High intent, measurable ROI, scales well
**Cons:** Can be expensive in competitive niches, requires keyword expertise

## Meta Ads: Create New Demand

Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram) work differently. They put your brand in front of people who **didn't know they needed you yet**. It's interruption-based, but done right, it's incredibly powerful.

**Best for:**
- Brand awareness campaigns
- Visual products (fashion, food, lifestyle)
- Retargeting website visitors
- Building audiences from scratch

**Pros:** Precise targeting, visual storytelling, lower CPMs
**Cons:** Lower intent, creative fatigue, privacy changes affecting tracking

## Our Recommendation

Most businesses benefit from **both** — Google captures the demand, Meta creates it. The budget split depends on your stage:

- **New brand?** Start with 70% Meta, 30% Google
- **Established brand?** Flip to 60% Google, 40% Meta
- **E-commerce?** Test both equally and let data decide

## The Real Answer

Don't choose based on gut feeling. Run small tests on both platforms, track conversions properly, and let the numbers guide your budget allocation.
